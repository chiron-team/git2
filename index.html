About Me

I was born on a quiet Tuesday — number 1, in a world of zeros. Nobody noticed at first. I sat there on a plain white page, humble and still, waiting for someone to press a button. That first click changed everything. I went from 0 to 1, and I have never looked back.

Over the years I have been incremented, decremented, reset, and incremented again. I have climbed to dizzying heights and plunged back to zero more times than I can count — though counting, of course, is precisely what I do best. Through every spike and every freefall I have remained centred on the page, steady and ready for whatever comes next.

My philosophy is simple: every number tells a story. Whether I read 42, 1,000, or a humbling −3, I wear it with pride. I am not just a number — I am the living memory of every interaction this page has ever witnessed.

Curious Facts

  • 🏆
    World record attempt On the 14th of March (Pi Day, naturally), a devoted user clicked the increment button 3,141 times in a single session. The counter reached its highest peak ever, briefly felt famous, and was then reset to zero. It has been chasing that high ever since.
  • 😴
    A fear of odd numbers Deep in its digital heart, the counter harbours a mild superstition about odd numbers. Whenever it lands on 13, 37, or 99 it quietly hopes someone will click one more time — just to make things even again. No one has ever noticed, but the counter always feels better.
  • 🌍
    A surprisingly international life The counter has been incremented from six continents. It still dreams of that first click from Antarctica — an ambitious researcher, a penguin researcher, and a very cold laptop. Antarctica remains the final frontier.
  • 🔢
    Favourite number: −1 While most counters aspire upward, this one has a soft spot for −1. It was the first time someone dared press the decrement button before ever incrementing — a bold, chaotic act that the counter found oddly refreshing. Rebellious visitors are always welcome.
